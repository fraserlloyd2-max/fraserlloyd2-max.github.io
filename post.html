Abu Dhabi Grand Prix Preview: Verstappen Targets the Greatest Comeback in F1 History

Another incredible Formula 1 season is reaching its conclusion, and anticipation couldn't be higher. Three drivers - Lando Norris, Max Verstappen and Oscar Piastri - enter the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix with a chance to be world champion, and remarkably, they do so level on seven wins each. With Norris leading on 408 points, Verstappen on 396 and Piastri on 392, the stage is set for one of the most intense championship finals we have ever seen. Every outcome is as big for the sport as the others. Will we see a maiden World Drivers Championship for either of the McLaren drivers or a record-breaking 5th in a row for Max Verstappen?

Since securing the Constructors Championship months ago in Singapore after mid-season domination, McLaren have had their sights on securing a driver's title for one of their men in Papaya, a first since Lewis Hamilton in 2007. The driver's title has refused to settle, with an early points lead taken by Oscar Piastri, which has swung the other way in Lando Norris' favour after his fine form since the summer break. However, few moments were bigger than Verstappen's clash with George Russell in Spain, a self-inflicted incident that cost the Dutchman a likely victory and may potentially cost him a 5th championship. Without it, this title fight could be even tighter, perhaps separated by a handful of points rather than double digits. Regardless of Sunday's result, it remains impressive that Verstappen is even in contention after being 104 points off the lead after his home grand prix in Zandvoort.

Verstappen and Norris have won the last 5 instalments of the Abu Dhabi with 4 and 1 respectively. Norris won the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix last year, with Verstappen 50 seconds behind in 6th and his teammate, Piastri, finishing 10th. Norris knows that any podium finish secures him the title, but the pressure of a first championship tilt is unlike anything he has previously faced. Piastri, just 16 points behind and arguably the sharpest qualifier in 2025 of the three, remains a genuine threat, especially if he can control the race from the front.

Strategy is expected to mirror recent years: a one-stop using medium and hard tyres, as it's projected to be the fastest option. A two-stop race is a viable, more aggressive option, especially if a safety car occurs, as we saw in the last blockbuster finale in Abu Dhabi in 2021. But regardless of how the race unfolds, one narrative towers above the rest: if Verstappen overturns the deficit here - after Spain, after the mid-season slump, being 104 points behind - it will be remembered as one of the greatest comebacks in Formula 1 history.
